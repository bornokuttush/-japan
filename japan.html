What is Sumo?

Sumo is a traditional Japanese sport where two wrestlers, called rikishi, compete to force each other out of a ring or to make the other touch the ground. Sumo is a combination of athletic skill, mental discipline, and cultural ritual.

History of Sumo

Sumo dates back over 1,500 years and was originally a religious ceremony to pray for a good harvest. It evolved into the sport we know today, with a rich tradition of rituals and ceremonies.

Sumo Rules

The goal of a sumo match is simple: to force your opponent out of the ring or make any part of their body, except the soles of their feet, touch the ground. Matches are usually very quick, with many lasting less than a minute.

Top Rikishi

Top rikishi are sumo wrestlers who achieve the highest rank of Yokozuna. Some of the most famous names in sumo include Hakuho, Asashoryu, and Kisenosato. These wrestlers have demonstrated incredible skill and mental toughness throughout their careers.
